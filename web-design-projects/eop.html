Project:

Edge of Park

Scope:

Since 1968 Edge of Park Bicycle and Moped Rental has operated as a family run business providing the ideal way to experience Peninsula State Park. When ownership changed in 2015, the new owners wanted to step up their digital presence by redesigning their website.

Skills:
  • Layout Design
  • Typography
  • Web Design

My Process

The Homepage

Prior to the kickoff meeting with the client, I had sent them a questionnaire asking about their vision and brand. I had asked specifics on imagery, style, font, colors and more. I wanted to keep the branding from the logo and pair it with nature-styled graphics/texture to tie in their beautiful location.

EoP had some wonderful photos taken showcasing visitors riding their rentals in the beautiful backdrop of Door County, WI. Door County is one of the most unique ecological areas in the country. Trails and roads within Peninsula State Park lead you along the Niagara Escarpment, a geological foundation of glacial remains that stretches from the northern parts of Illinois all the way to Niagara Falls. Growing within the Cedar and Beech forests that stretch along its shoreline you can discover unique foliage and wildlife. It truly is a place like no other and I wanted the site to reflect this natural feel.

My goal was to keep the homepage simple and let the images speak for themselves. The customer's main goal was to create a quick and easy way for visitors to book their rentals. With this goal in mind and an approved homepage mockup, I designed the mobile and internal mockups too.

Mobile & Interior

For the mobile and internal mockup, I used design elements from the homepage and trimmed down the content to really focus on the necessary CTAs. On each internal page, there is a left navigation that allows visitors to quickly access booking and nearby activities.

After the developers built the site, I would review the site and take detailed notes of any flaws/mistakes and work with developers to resolve them. Once the site was pixel perfect to my mockup, I would send the customer a php link to look over the different mocks alongside a detailed explanation of each section on the site.

When we received final approval from the customer, our project manager would provide a course on how to use the content management system (Wordpress). From there, the customer was in control of keeping their content accurate and updated.

This project brought me a lot of pride and joy. It wasn't often that I was able to work with such beautiful material. The client loved the final product and said it truly showcased everything they had to offer at Edge of Park.
